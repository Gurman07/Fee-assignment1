Brief about the book

1.

 The Book of Lost Friends Lisa Wingate "The Book of Lost Friends" by Lisa Wingate is a gripping historical fiction novel set in Louisiana. It intricately weaves together the lives of characters across two timelines: the Reconstruction era and present-day. Through powerful storytelling, Wingate explores themes of loss, resilience, and the enduring power of hope across generations.

2.

 An Orphan's War Molly Green "An Orphan's War" by Molly Green portrays the poignant struggles of orphaned children during World War II. Set against the backdrop of conflict, the novel delves into their resilience, bonds of friendship, and the enduring human spirit amidst adversity, offering a heartfelt narrative of survival and hope.

3.

 The Creative Thinking Handbook Chris Griffiths "The Creative Thinking Handbook" by Chris Griffiths is a practical guide to enhance creativity and innovation. It explores techniques like mind mapping, brainstorming, and lateral thinking to generate and develop ideas. The book encourages a structured yet flexible approach to problem-solving and creativity in personal and professional contexts.

4.

 Positioning for Professionals Tim Williams "Positioning for Professionals" by Tim Williams is a guide for professional service firms on differentiating themselves in a competitive market. It covers identifying unique value propositions, developing strong positioning statements, and effectively communicating brand identity, with practical strategies, case studies, and advice on implementation and overcoming challenges.

5.

 Rich Dad Poor Dad Robert T. Kiyosaki "Rich Dad Poor Dad" by Robert T. Kiyosaki is a personal finance classic. It contrasts the financial philosophies of the author's "poor dad" (his biological father) and "rich dad" (his friend's father), emphasizing principles like investing in assets, financial education, and challenging conventional beliefs about wealth and success.

6.

 Breadcrumbs Anne Ursu "Breadcrumbs" by Anne Ursu is a modern adaptation of the classic fairy tale "The Snow Queen". It follows the story of Hazel, a young girl who must embark on a magical and perilous journey to rescue her best friend Jack, who has been taken by the Snow Queen.

7.

 How to Stop Worrying and Start Living Dale Carnegie "How to Stop Worrying and Start Living" by Dale Carnegie offers practical strategies to manage stress and anxiety. It emphasizes living in the present, tackling problems systematically, and cultivating a positive mindset. Through anecdotes and actionable advice, Carnegie guides readers to overcome worry and lead a more productive and peaceful life.

8.

 Think and Grow Rich Napoleon Hill "Think and Grow Rich" by Napoleon Hill is a classic self-help book focused on achieving success and wealth. It outlines principles based on interviews with successful individuals, emphasizing the power of mindset, persistence, and goal-setting. The book advocates for harnessing the subconscious mind to attain financial and personal prosperity.

9.

 The Long Awakening Lindsey O'Connor "The Long Awakening" by Lindsey O'Connor is a memoir recounting the author's personal journey through a coma and recovery. It explores her profound spiritual experiences, challenges in rediscovering life post-coma, and the impact on her faith and relationships. The narrative reflects on resilience, hope, and the power of faith.

10.

 The Lives We Lost Megan Crewe "The Lives We Lost" by Megan Crewe continues the dystopian journey of Kaelyn and her community as they struggle against a deadly virus. With tensions rising and resources dwindling, they must navigate through societal breakdown and personal sacrifices to survive in a world forever changed by the pandemic.

11.

 I Can and I Will: Mindset of Success William H. McRaven "I Can and I Will: Mindset of Success" by William H. McRaven offers motivational insights drawn from the author's experiences as a Navy SEAL and leader. It emphasizes resilience, determination, and the power of a positive mindset in overcoming challenges and achieving personal and professional goals.

12.

 A Thousand Pieces of You Claudia Gray A Thousand Pieces of You" by Claudia Gray is a sci-fi romance where Marguerite travels through parallel universes to avenge her father's murder. Amid love and betrayal, she uncovers truths about herself and her family, challenging her perceptions of reality and destiny in a thrilling multiverse adventure.
